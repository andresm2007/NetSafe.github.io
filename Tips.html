Increase your security by auditing your passwords:

Make sure your passwords have a strong character mix and update them constantly.
Avoid common passwords such as 1234, abcde, password, etc.


Update your browsers:

It is very important to keep our browsers and antivirus systems up to date.
Of course, when you update your browsers, do it from the provider's official website.


Avoid using public WI-FI

By using them, we are exposing our data, traffic, and identity. If you have no choice but to use one, when you connect, indicate that this connection is to a public network (and not a work or home network) and thus prevent your folders or resources from being available to anyone.


Download only what is necessary

If you don't have to download something, don't. It is common for hackers to hide malware in email attachments, but you can also download something you shouldn't online.



Antivirus

Download and install a virus scanner for added security.


Remove malicious applications

Find and remove malicious applications you find or have on your device.


Google Play Protect

Activate Google Play Protect for increased security when downloading something from Google.


Using anti-malware software

The best way to automatically remove viruses and malware from your phone is by using anti-malware software applications.



If you suspect, act with caution

If something seems suspicious to you, such as an email, an alert, a website, a link... Before clicking or taking any action on the element, analyze the situation and act with caution.


Stay informed

Almost as important as updating software is keeping yourself up to date with the latest cybersecurity news.
Pay attention to cybersecurity news to stay up to date and minimize risks.


Don't be fooled into clicking on links/attachments.

With the sheer volume of emails arriving in your inbox every day, it's easy to be tricked into clicking on an attachment or link that looks like it was sent by someone you know.


Save everything on a shared drive/cloud

You should try to work from an online platform such as Microsoft SharePoint, or at least back up all your work regularly to a cloud service, to avoid losing it in the unfortunate event of a ransomware attack. Don't rely on or work from your desktop computer, as it can fail at any time.
